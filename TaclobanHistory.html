  • History

Tacloban City, located on the island of Leyte in the Philippines, has a rich history dating back to the 16th century. The city was originally settled by the Waray people, an indigenous group native to the region. In the 17th century, the Spanish colonized the area and established a settlement in what is now Tacloban City. During the Spanish colonial period, the city served as an important center for trade and commerce.

In 1898, the Philippines became a colony of the United States following the Spanish-American War. Tacloban City was designated as the capital of the newly created Leyte province. During World War II, the city was occupied by Japanese forces and was heavily damaged by Allied bombing.

On November 8, 2013, Tacloban City was devastated by super typhoon Haiyan (known as Yolanda in the Philippines), which killed thousands of people and left many more homeless. The city has since been rebuilt and continues to be a major economic and cultural center in the region.
